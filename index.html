Fast bowler

king of swing

Anderson was the first English bowler to reach 400, 500 and 600 wickets in Test matches. As of January 2021 he is ranked 6th in the ICC Test Bowling Rankings,[6] having previously reached the top position at various times between 2016 and 2018.

Experience

Test career

international test debut

In the summer of 2003, he made his first Test cricket appearance against Zimbabwe at Lord's, and took five wickets in an innings on debut,[32] becoming the 42nd Englishman to do so. His success continued in the subsequent One-Day tournaments against Pakistan (against whom he took a hat-trick at The Oval), South Africa and Zimbabwe. His hat-trick against Pakistan, the first by an English bowler in an ODI, claimed the wickets of Abdul Razzaq, Shoaib Akhtar and Mohammad Sami.[33]

Odi career

One day international debut

When Anderson was selected for the England one-day squad, he had played only five List A matches, taking 23 wickets at an average of 26.75.[29] At the age of 20, he made his ODI debut on 15 December 2002 against Australia at Melbourne.
